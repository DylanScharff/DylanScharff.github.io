Challenges & Feedback

While my overall experience with TryHackMe has been highly positive, my interaction with the SAL1 certification exam introduced some significant challenges that are worth documenting in detail. I share this not as a critique of the learning platform itself, but as feedback on a specific assessment experience that impacted my certification path.

I initially enrolled in the SAL1 exam with the intention of using it as a final step before applying for entry-level roles in cybersecurity. During my first attempt, I encountered critical technical issues within the SOC simulation environment, particularly in sections two and three. These issues prevented normal interaction with the lab, including the ability to process tickets and generate reports, effectively making it impossible to complete the assessment.

After reporting the issue, I engaged with the support team and provided detailed documentation, including screenshots and screen recordings, to help clarify the problem. I was offered a retake after initial communication, along with assurances that the issue had been addressed.

However, upon retaking the exam, I experienced the same core problem again in the SOC simulation environment. At this point, I continued correspondence with support in more detail, further explaining the behavior and impact of the issue on exam completion, and troubleshooting possible issues on my end. Following this, communication from the support side stopped entirely for over a week without clear updates or resolution.

Later, I received a message indicating that updates had been deployed and that the issue should be resolved after clearing cookies and re-authenticating. Despite following these instructions, the same problem persisted during my attempt. Reaching out to support again, I have recieved no response for more than two weeks now.

As a result, I have been unable to complete the certification despite multiple attempts and extended troubleshooting efforts. This has delayed my ability to present a formal certification alongside the practical skills I have developed through hands-on SOC-style lab work and independent study.

Outside of this experience, my engagement with TryHackMe’s learning paths has remained consistently valuable. It is worth noting that this feedback is specific to the certification exam environment rather than the platform’s educational content, which I continue to use and benefit from.

Side note: some estimated completion times for individual rooms can be highly inaccurate, but this has had minimal impact on the overall learning experience.

Conclusion

Overall, TryHackMe has been a valuable and ongoing part of my cybersecurity learning journey. I plan to continue using the platform to strengthen my skills, explore advanced topics, and reinforce practical knowledge in preparation for future roles in the cybersecurity field.
